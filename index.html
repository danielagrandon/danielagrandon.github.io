I am a physicist specializing in the intersection of theoretical cosmology and data science. Currently, I serve as a postdoctoral research associate and lecturer at Leiden University, where my work focuses on the statistical characterization of non-Gaussian cosmic fields and the development of high-precision inference methods for cosmological parameters.

My research explores Bayesian frameworks and machine learning-based inference using late-time cosmological probes, to elucidate fundamental physics about the Universe, such as the nature of dark energy, dark matter and inflation. I utilize real data from galaxy cataloges, and state-of-the-art cosmological simulations. I co-lead the Higher Order Statistics Topical Team within LSST-DESC, I co-lead the Data Release 1 Euclid cosmological analysis using weak lensing higher-order statistics, and I am a timezone representative of the International Astrostatistics Association.

In 2022, I was honored with the L'Oréal-UNESCO "For Women in Science" Prize in Chile for my contributions to cosmology and data science.

Bio

I hold a PhD in Physics from the Universidad de Chile, where my work focused on novel statistical approaches and machine learning techniques to analyzing the weak lensing field and constraining dark energy models. During my PhD I spent 3 months as a visiting researcher at Kavli IPMU, Japan.

Prior to my PhD, I obtained my undergraduate degree in Physics at Instituto de Física y Astronomía (UV) in the beautiful city of Valparaíso, a UNESCO World Heritage site. My research at the undergraduate level mostly focused on stellar astrophysics and theoretical cosmology.

Currently I am a postdoc at the Mathematical Institute of Leiden University in Dr. Elena Sellentin's group. My research focuses on Bayesian frameworks to mitigate the impact of systematics on cosmological probes, to leverage the constraining power of Stage IV cosmological surveys.

I hold leadership roles in multiple physics collaborations, including Rubin LSST-DESC, the Euclid consortia, the CosmoVerse COST action and the International Astrostatistics Association.

Beyond research, I am an advocate for diversity in STEM, actively mentoring young researchers and promoting scientific outreach. Despite living abroad, I constantly organize in-person outreach activities in my hometown, Villa Alemana (German village, in english, also known as the city of mills). In 2023, I was named a Distinguished Citizen by the Municipality's council.
