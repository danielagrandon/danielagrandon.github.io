About Me

I am a physicist specializing in the intersection of theoretical cosmology and data science. Currently, I serve as a postdoctoral research associate and lecturer at Leiden University, where my work focuses on the statistical characterization of non-Gaussian cosmic fields and the development of high-precision inference methods for cosmological parameters.

My research explores Bayesian frameworks and machine learning-based inference using late-time cosmological probes, to elucidate fundamental physics about the Universe, such as the nature of dark energy, dark matter and inflation. I co-lead the Higher Order Statistics Topical Team within LSST-DESC and co-lead the Data Release 1 Euclid cosmological analysis using weak lensing higher-order statistics.

In 2022, I was honored with the L'Oréal-UNESCO "For Women in Science" Prize in Chile for my contributions to cosmology and data science.

Biography

Dr. Daniela Grandón is a theoretical cosmologist with a strong background in statistical inference and data science. She obtained her PhD in Physics from the University of Chile, where her work focused on novel approaches to analyzing cosmic structures using higher-order statistics.

Her postdoctoral research at Leiden University involves leading efforts in weak gravitational lensing studies, collaborating on the LSST-DESC and Euclid projects to refine cosmological parameter estimation techniques. She has also contributed extensively to machine learning applications in astrophysics.

Beyond research, Dr. Grandón is an advocate for diversity in STEM, actively mentoring young researchers and promoting scientific outreach. Her accolades include the L'Oréal-UNESCO "For Women in Science" Prize, recognizing her outstanding contributions to the field.
